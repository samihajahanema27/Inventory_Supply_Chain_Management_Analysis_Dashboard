#  Inventory & Supply Chain Management Analysis Dashboard

An interactive **Power BI dashboard** developed to analyze key inventory and supply chain performance metrics. This project transforms raw operational data into meaningful business insights using **Power Query**, **Data Modeling**, and **DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)**.

---

##  Project Overview

Efficient inventory and supply chain management are essential for reducing operational costs, improving warehouse utilization, and ensuring timely order fulfillment. This dashboard provides a centralized view of critical KPIs, enabling users to monitor business performance and identify opportunities for improvement.

The dashboard is fully interactive, allowing users to filter and analyze data by **Region** and **Category**.

---

##  Objectives

- Monitor warehouse utilization and inventory performance.
- Analyze transportation costs across different regions and product categories.
- Track inventory turnover and Days Sales of Inventory (DSI).
- Evaluate supplier lead times.
- Monitor order fulfillment and backorders.
- Support business decision-making through interactive visualizations.

---

##  DAX Measures Used

This dashboard uses several DAX measures to calculate business KPIs dynamically, including:

- Warehouse Utilization
- Inventory Turnover Ratio
- Days Sales of Inventory (DSI)
- Total Transportation Cost
- Total Units Sold
- Average Lead Time
- Total Inventory Level
- Backorder Count

These measures automatically update all visuals based on user-selected filters.

---

## 📈 Key Insights

- Warehouse utilization is approximately **34%**, indicating available storage capacity that could be better optimized.
- Inventory turnover ratio is approximately **23.47**, suggesting efficient inventory movement.
- Days Sales of Inventory averages around **15.56 days**, indicating relatively fast inventory turnover.
- Transportation costs vary across different regions and product categories, highlighting opportunities for logistics optimization.
- The majority of orders are successfully fulfilled, with comparatively fewer pending and canceled orders.
- Inventory levels remain relatively balanced across product categories.
- Variations in supplier lead time reveal opportunities for improving supply chain efficiency.
